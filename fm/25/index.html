Myth debunked

Millennials, Not Baby Boomers, Are the Driving Force Behind the Recent Surge in Apartment Demand

Popular notion holds that downsizing Baby Boomers are a major force behind the recent apartment boom. For example, one recent article noted that the “…magnitude of Boomers’ rental demand [has stunned] some industry observers”, while another asserted that, “…boomers are renting apartments and buying condos at more than twice the rate of their millennial children.”

The problem with these accounts is that they aren’t supported by the data. Click the buttons below ("The Myth", "The Reality" and "The Bottom Line") to see what's going on...

The Reality: Millennials are the driving force; Boomers are aging in place. Click "The Bottom Line" button (above) to compare the two perspectives.

Bottom Line: Millennials, not Baby Boomers are the driving force. Click (link to Pat's commentary) for the full story.

The data: A lot of articles use bar charts like the one on the right to rationalize the myth that Baby Boomers drove a surge in apartment rentals (almost 1 million) between 2009 and 2014. That's not what the data are saying.

The problem: This bar chart uses fixed age ranges. Baby Boomers were 49 to 68 years old in 2014, but not in 2009. Betwen 2009 and 2014, the generations age 5 years but the bars in the chart do not.

Click the year buttons (above) to review the change over time.

The data: The bars in the chart (at left) change their age range along with the generations. This bar chart tells a very different story. The data are the same as used to rationalize the myth.

What the data say: Millennials drove a huge increase in rental demand from 2009 to 2014 (over 2.2 million). In comparison, Baby Boomers account for only a small increase (211 thousand). The chart also shows that Generation X and Older Americans left the market during that time.
