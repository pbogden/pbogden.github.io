Recent history

The dynamic graphic below shows the recent growth in Oklahoma earthquakes attributed to wastewater disposal that occurs when drilling for oil and gas (including fracking).

Click "Start" to see the evolution of earthquakes. You can also drag the filter (translucent gray rectangle) left and right with your mouse.

You can see an apparent growth in Oklahoma earthquakes since the beginning 2013.

However, the next graphic shows that there's a problem with this interpretation of the data. The bars represent the # of monthly earthquakes for the continental US (blue) and Oklahoma (orange). There's a spike in the # of earthquakes in early 2010, and generally more earthquakes after 2013. The monthly values for Oklahoma also seem to high when compared to the USGS press release.
