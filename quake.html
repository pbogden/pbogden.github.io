Recent history

The dynamic graphic below shows the time history of earthquakes. The data come from the USGS Earthquake Web Service API.

Click "Start" to see the evolution of earthquakes. You can also drag the filter (translucent gray rectangle) left and right with your mouse.

If you focus on Oklahoma, you can see dramatric growth in the number of earthquakes beginning in 2012/2013.

A dramatic increase in Oklahoma earthquakes known to be associated with oil and gad drilling (including fracking). However, there's a problem with this interpretation of the data shown above...

The next graphic shows the # of earthquakes each month for the continental US (blue) and Oklahoma (orange). Two features are noteworthy: (1) a spike in total earthquakes in early 2010, and (2) a sharp increase in earthquakes after 2013.

The values for Oklahoma also seem to high when compared to the USGS press release.
